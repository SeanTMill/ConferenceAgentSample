Novel Loss Functions for Sparse Mixture-of-Experts Models in Low-Resource Settings

Research Track Deep Learning
Oct 30th, 11:00 AM - 11:45 AM | Room 401 (Academic Track)
Dr. Amara Singh, Assistant Professor, CSAIL at North American Technical University (NATU)
Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architectures offer computational efficiency but often suffer from training instability in low-resource environments. We propose a novel auxiliary loss function that balances expert load more effectively than current state-of-the-art baselines. We present empirical results across multiple benchmarks demonstrating a 15% improvement in convergence speed and superior downstream task performance on diverse languages.

Federated Learning Benchmarks: A Comparative Analysis of Aggregation Strategies under Non-IID Data Distributions

Research Track Privacy Preserving AI
Oct 30th, 1:00 PM - 1:45 PM | Room 401 (Academic Track)
Jonas Weber, PhD Candidate at Berlin Institute for Open Research
As privacy concerns grow, Federated Learning (FL) is gaining traction. However, standard benchmarks often assume Independent and Identically Distributed (IID) data, which rarely reflects real-world scenarios. This talk presents a rigorous comparative analysis of aggregation strategies (FedAvg, FedProx, SCAFFOLD) under severe non-IID conditions, offering new guidelines for robust FL deployment.

Detecting and Mitigating Subtle Hallucinations in Multi-Modal Agentic Workflows

Research Track AI Safety
Oct 30th, 2:00 PM - 2:45 PM | Room 401 (Academic Track)
Dr. Fatima Al-Fassi, Senior Research Fellow at Oxford Centre for AI Ethics
When agents interact with both text and images, hallucination modes become complex and interdependent. Our research uncovers how visual inputs can sometimes override textual ground truth in current VLM architectures. We introduce a new evaluation framework, 'GroundTruth-MM', for systematically stress-testing multi-modal agents against subtle, dangerous fabrication errors.
